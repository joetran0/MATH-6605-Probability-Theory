MATH 6605 Probability Theory (Winter 2025)

Overview

This course examines the essentials of probability theory from the rigorous mathematical viewpoint. Topics include basic measure theory, the weak and strong laws of large numbers, the Borel-Canteli Lemma, zero-one laws, the law of the iterated logarithm, convergence in probability and weak convergence, characteristic functions, the central limit theorem, conditional probability and expectation, and martingales.

Prerequisites

An undergraduate course in probability is required. An undergraduate course in analysis will be useful. A previous course in measure theory is not necessary, since we will introduce the measure theory that we need.

Textbook/Resources

  • Course notes
  • A First Look at Rigorous Probability, by Jeffery S. Rosenthal. World Scientific, 2006.

Tentative Material

  1. Probability spaces: \(\sigma\)-algebras and measures (Chapter 1, 2)
  2. Random variables and their distributions (Chapter 3, 6)
  3. Expected Value (Chapter 4)
  4. Convergence of random variables; various kinds of convergence (Chapter 5, 10)
  5. Limit theorems
  6. Characteristic functions (Chapter 11)
  7. Conditional probability and expectation (Chapter 13)
  8. Martingales (Chapter 14)
